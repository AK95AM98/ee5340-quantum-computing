# Concept Taxonomy

This document describes the 16 taxonomy categories used to classify the 200 concepts in the EE 5340 learning graph.

## Taxonomy Categories

### MATH -- Mathematical Foundations
**Concepts 1-15 (15 concepts)**

Core mathematical tools required throughout the course including complex numbers, linear algebra, matrix operations, eigenvalue theory, and probability. These concepts form the prerequisite foundation for quantum mechanics, information theory, and algorithm analysis.

### PLIM -- Physical Limits
**Concepts 16-28 (13 concepts)**

Fundamental physical constraints on computation including thermodynamic entropy, Landauer's principle, Moore's Law and its limits, and ultimate computational bounds such as the Margolus-Levitin theorem, Bremermann's limit, and the Bekenstein bound.

### INFO -- Information Theory
**Concepts 29-41 (13 concepts)**

Shannon's information theory framework covering entropy measures, mutual information, channel capacity, coding theorems, and algorithmic information theory. These concepts bridge classical and quantum approaches to information processing.

### CLAS -- Classical Computing
**Concepts 42-53 (12 concepts)**

Classical digital computing fundamentals from Boolean algebra and logic gates through CMOS transistor physics, power dissipation mechanisms, the Boltzmann tyranny, and computational complexity theory including the Church-Turing thesis.

### BCMOS -- Beyond-CMOS Devices
**Concepts 54-63 (10 concepts)**

Emerging device technologies that aim to overcome CMOS scaling limitations including spintronics, memristors, tunnel FETs, negative capacitance FETs, and ferroelectric materials. These represent alternative approaches to extending computing performance.

### RVRSE -- Reversible Computing
**Concepts 64-73 (10 concepts)**

Reversible computation theory and practice covering logical and physical reversibility, Bennett's proof, reversible gates (Toffoli, Fredkin), adiabatic computing, energy recovery logic, and the management of garbage bits.

### QMECH -- Quantum Mechanics
**Concepts 74-89 (16 concepts)**

Foundational quantum mechanical principles including wave-particle duality, superposition, Dirac notation, Hilbert space formalism, quantum measurement, the Born rule, density matrices, the Schrodinger equation, and the no-cloning theorem.

### QSTATE -- Qubits and States
**Concepts 90-103 (14 concepts)**

Qubit representations and multi-qubit quantum states including the Bloch sphere, computational basis states, quantum entanglement, Bell states, the EPR paradox, Bell's inequality, Schmidt decomposition, and partial trace operations.

### QGATE -- Quantum Gates
**Concepts 104-119 (16 concepts)**

Quantum gate operations from single-qubit Pauli and Hadamard gates through multi-qubit CNOT and controlled gates to universal gate sets, gate decomposition, the Solovay-Kitaev theorem, magic state distillation, and the quantum circuit model.

### QALG -- Quantum Algorithms
**Concepts 120-137 (18 concepts)**

Major quantum algorithms and their components including quantum parallelism, oracles, the Deutsch-Jozsa family, quantum Fourier transform, phase estimation, Shor's algorithm, Grover's search with amplitude amplification, and quantum walks.

### QERR -- Error Correction
**Concepts 138-152 (15 concepts)**

Quantum error correction theory and codes covering noise channels, Kraus operators, bit-flip and phase-flip errors, repetition codes, the Shor and Steane codes, stabilizer formalism, surface codes, logical qubits, and fault-tolerant thresholds.

### QHWRE -- Quantum Hardware
**Concepts 153-166 (14 concepts)**

Physical implementations of quantum computers including superconducting qubits (transmon, Josephson junction), trapped ion systems, photonic qubits, topological qubits, quantum dot qubits, and supporting infrastructure such as dilution refrigerators.

### STOCH -- Stochastic Computing
**Concepts 167-176 (10 concepts)**

Stochastic computing paradigm covering stochastic number encoding, bit stream operations (multiplication, addition), MUX-based logic, variance analysis, correlation effects, decorrelation techniques, error tolerance, and applications in image processing.

### HYBRID -- Hybrid Computing
**Concepts 177-186 (10 concepts)**

Hybrid quantum-classical algorithms and near-term quantum computing approaches including the variational quantum eigensolver (VQE), QAOA, parameterized quantum circuits, barren plateaus, NISQ era constraints, quantum error mitigation, and zero-noise extrapolation.

### ENRGY -- Energy-Efficient Computing
**Concepts 187-194 (8 concepts)**

Alternative computing paradigms focused on energy efficiency including neuromorphic computing, spiking neural networks, approximate computing, in-memory and near-memory computing, thermodynamic computing, and analog neural accelerators.

### FUTRE -- Future of Computing
**Concepts 195-200 (6 concepts)**

Assessment of the current state and future directions of computing including quantum supremacy experiments, quantum advantage, benchmarking metrics (cross-entropy benchmarking, quantum volume), quantum resource estimation, and the post-Moore computing landscape.
